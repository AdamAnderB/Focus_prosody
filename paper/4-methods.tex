2.1 Participants
A total of 120 participants took part in this study, divided into three groups: native English speakers (N = 40), Cantonese learners of English (N = 40), and Dutch learners of English (N = 40). All participants had normal or corrected-to-normal vision and hearing, provided informed consent, and were compensated for their time.
Half of the native English speakers (N = 20) were undergraduate students at a North American university, while the remaining (N = 20) were recruited via Prolific. Following Brown et al. (2023)’s definition of native speakers, all English participants were born and raised in an English-speaking environment, with English as their first and primary language.
The Cantonese (N = 40) and Dutch (N = 35) speakers were fully recruited via Prolific. All reported Cantonese or Dutch as their first and primary language.
To ensure consistency, participants completed a detailed language background questionnaire, confirming age of L2 acquisition, language exposure history, and self-rated proficiency. For all language groups, participants had no significant early exposure to other languages at home, and completed primary and secondary education in either English-, Cantonese- or Dutch-speaking environments, respectively.
All participants completed the LexTALE, a validated measure of English proficiency. Scores were used to confirm comparable proficiency levels across L2 groups. While Cantonese learners acquired English earlier and had more years of exposure, Dutch learners scored slightly higher on LexTALE, consistent with previous research showing greater lexical transfer effects in Germanic languages (Lemhöfer & Broersma, 2012). A total of 12 participants were excluded from the study due to language background factors. Specifically, 7 Cantonese speakers were removed for reporting significant early exposure to Mandarin, either through home use or early schooling. Similarly, 5 Dutch speakers were excluded due to high levels of exposure to Frisian or German from early childhood. These participants were removed to ensure that the remaining sample consisted of monolingual Cantonese and Dutch speakers who acquired English as a second language.
In addition, 5 participants were excluded for methodological reasons. Two participants were removed due to not passing headphone checks or unresponsive eye-tracking calibration.
